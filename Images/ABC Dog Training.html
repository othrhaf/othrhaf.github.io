Why Clicker Train Your Dog?

We need to communicate to our dogs when they are doing something we like. A verbal reward, marker word, or a clicker. People new to training are sometimes intimated by the aspect of using a clicker. I believe that it is because of the management of it all. We have the dog on leash, we need to cue the dog as to what behavior we are asking for (watching closely for the dog to comply), marking it and delivery of the treat. It seems overwhelming to the new trainer. Humans being verbal, think that as long as they use the same word or phrase, it should mean the same for the dog. First, dogs don’t speak English. They primarily speak with their body. So, it isn’t the words used, for the dog it is just a 'sound' we two legs make.


Unfortunately, the 'sound' doesn’t always sound the same. Our voices can say the same word in different ways, and so express different emotions or meanings each time. If we are excited, tired, or frustrated, it is conveyed with the tone. Can you imagine how difficult it must be for a dog to pick out a single sound from the stream of meaningless sounds they hear us speak every day? Can you see how confusing that can be for the dog?
